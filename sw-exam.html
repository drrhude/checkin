Shockwave Therapy Informed Consent
What is Shockwave Therapy? Radial Pressure Wave Therapy (RPWT) uses the PINZHIMED PZ-3201M to deliver high-energy acoustic waves into injured tissue. It is non-invasive and drug-free.

During Treatment: You will feel deep tapping or pressure — moderately uncomfortable. A 6–7/10 discomfort level is normal and therapeutic.

After Treatment (24–72 hrs): Expect local soreness. This is a normal healing response. Avoid NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) for 48 hours. Light movement encouraged; avoid aggressive loading 48 hrs.

Typical Course: 3–10 sessions, 1–2x per week. Most improvement after sessions 3–4. Results vary.

Risks: Temporary soreness, bruising, skin sensitivity. Serious adverse events rare with proper screening.

By signing, I confirm I have read this, had the opportunity to ask questions, and voluntarily consent to shockwave therapy.
